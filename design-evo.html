AEROpong Design Evolution

Our Mechanical Design Journey

From initial handheld concepts to our final desktop pull-spring launcher, we explored multiple mechanisms to achieve the perfect ping pong ball training system.

Handheld Motor-Spring

  • Initial Concept: Portable handheld launcher
  • Advantages: Mobile, compact design
  • Challenges: Difficult to aim consistently, required power source
  • Transition Point: Realized need for stable platform during testing

Desktop Wheel System

  • Second Iteration: Stationary tabletop unit
  • Advantages: Stable platform, consistent shots
  • Challenges: Complex motor control, limited power adjustment
  • Key Insight: Desktop format improved accuracy significantly

Pneumatic Desktop

  • Alternative Approach: Air-powered desktop unit
  • Advantages: Powerful launch, adjustable pressure
  • Challenges: Required compressor, noisy operation
  • Learning: Simpler mechanisms might be more reliable

Final Pull-Spring Desktop

  • Optimal Solution: Manual pull-spring desktop launcher
  • Key Features:
    • Simple pull mechanism for power adjustment
    • Stable desktop base for consistent aiming
    • Spring tension customization
    • power required
  • Advantages: Reliable, adjustable, low-maintenance
  • Innovation: Combines simplicity with precision engineering
Our journey from handheld to desktop and from complex motorized systems to our elegant pull-spring mechanism taught us valuable lessons about engineering simplicity. The desktop configuration provided the stability needed for consistent training sessions, while the pull-spring design offered the perfect balance of user control and mechanical reliability. This evolution reflects our growing understanding that the most effective solutions often come from refining rather than complicating. As student engineers, we learned to value maintainability and user experience just as much as technical performance.
